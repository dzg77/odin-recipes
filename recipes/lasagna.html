Lasagna Recipe

Description

This homemade lasagna is the ultimate comfort food, layering rich and savory marinara sauce, creamy ricotta cheese, perfectly seasoned ground beef, and melty mozzarella. The key to a delicious lasagna is in the layers — each one contributing to a harmonious blend of flavors. A hearty meat sauce simmers with garlic, onions, and herbs, then gets sandwiched between tender lasagna noodles, followed by a generous spread of creamy ricotta and mozzarella. Baked to golden perfection, the top layer is slightly crispy, while the inside remains bubbling and gooey. Whether served for a family dinner or a gathering with friends, this lasagna is sure to be a crowd-pleaser.

This recipe also allows for some flexibility — you can make it your own by adding vegetables like spinach or mushrooms, or by swapping out the ground beef for ground turkey or plant-based options. The lasagna can even be made ahead of time and stored in the fridge, making it a great dish to prep for busy days. Paired with a fresh salad and garlic bread, it is a meal that is sure to satisfy all appetites.

Ingredients

  • 12 lasagna sheets
  • 1 lb ground beef
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 (24 oz) jar marinara sauce
  • 1 (15 oz) container ricotta cheese
  • 2 cups shredded mozzarella cheese
  • 1/2 cup grated parmesan cheese
  • 1 large egg
  • 2 teaspoons dried basil
  • 1 teaspoon dried oregano
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh basil (optional, for garnish)

Steps

  1. Preheat the oven to 375°F (190°C).
  2. Cook the lasagna noodles according to package instructions, drain, and set aside.
  3. In a large pan, brown the ground beef over medium heat, breaking it up with a spoon. Add chopped onion and minced garlic, cooking until softened, about 5 minutes.
  4. Stir in the marinara sauce, dried basil, oregano, and salt and pepper. Simmer for 10-15 minutes to allow the flavors to blend.
  5. In a separate bowl, mix the ricotta cheese, egg, and Parmesan. Season with a pinch of salt and pepper.
  6. Assemble the lasagna: Spread a thin layer of the meat sauce on the bottom of a 9x13-inch baking dish. Add a layer of lasagna noodles, then a layer of the ricotta mixture, followed by a layer of mozzarella. Repeat the layers, finishing with a layer of meat sauce and mozzarella on top.
  7. Cover with foil (to prevent sticking, you can spray the foil with cooking spray) and bake for 30 minutes.
  8. Remove the foil and bake for an additional 10-15 minutes, or until the cheese is bubbly and golden brown.
  9. Let the lasagna rest for 10 minutes before serving. Garnish with fresh basil if desired. Enjoy!
